Stagg EKG Review

You are here: Home / Equipment / Brewing / Stagg EKG Review

The Stagg EKG is one of the latest products from Fellow, a coffee device company dedicated to improving the way people brew coffee at home. Fellow is well-known for their wonderfully designed and incredibly functional coffee brewing apparatuses.

The Stagg EKG is an electric pour-over kettle complete with temperature selection, Fahrenheit-to-Celsius conversion, and capable of holding a chosen temperature for up to 60 minutes. Each Stagg EKG has an integrated PID controller to efficiently approach your desired brewing temperature.

The spout is designed just right to prevent the kettle from dripping and allowing you to achieve the perfect pour-over stream. We think the Stagg EKG is the perfect pour-over kettle for the craft coffee enthusiast.

Pros

  • Beautiful aesthetics – As will all other Fellow products, the Stagg EKG is the perfect kettle for your minimalist kitchen
  • To-the-Degree Temperature Selection – The Stagg EKG has a control knob on the base that lets you select temperatures from 135-212° F (57-100° C)
  • Quick heat-up – The unit uses a whopping 1200 watts of power to get your water from fridge-temp to brewing temp
  • Hold Temperatures – The system can hold a selected temperature for 60 minutes before it shuts the heating element off for safety

Cons

  • Temperature display – The temperature display screen feels a bit outdated to us and we aren’t in love with the pixelated style
  • Pricey – This electric kettle is definitely not the least expensive on the market, but we assure you it’s worth every penny
  • No cord storage – Lots of electric kettles on the market have a way to store the long cables that are attached to the base; we like to hide our cord so as to not detract from the minimalism of the kettle

Features

The Stagg EKG is chock full of features that we think make it an amazing pour-over kettle for the home barista or coffee enthusiast.

Temperature Adjustment

To start, one of the best things about this kettle is that it has temperature selection. The knob on the corner of the base is used to adjust the temperature from 135° F to 212° F (57-100° C). We love this adjustability because it lets us brew everything from AeroPress to pour-over coffee without losing cup quality by scalding the beans.

Brew Stopwatch

The kettle has a built-in brew timer that works like a charm. The temperature adjustment knob also doubles as a button to start the brew timer. When you’re ready to start it, you simply push and hold the knob down for 2 seconds. To stop the timer, you press the knob down one more time. We like this feature because timing your pour overs is one of the easiest ways to increase repeatability between cups.

Temperature Conversion

The Stagg EKG base is capable of switching between Fahrenheit and Celsius at the drop of a hat. All it takes is toggling the F/C switch on the back of the unit. When you flip between them, the screen will update to show you which unit you’re currently using. While you shouldn’t have to use this very often, it’s nice to leave the calculator in your desk while you brew coffee.

Digital Temperature Display

One of the novel upgrades that the Stagg EKG brought over the Stagg (in addition to being electric) is that it sports a digital temperature display, while the original Stagg uses an analog temperature dial. The EKG lets you achieve incredible precision because it tracks temperature on the digital display to a single degree. We really like being able to see the exact temperature the water is at as it heats up.

Holding Temperatures

Most people brew their coffee right after watching their water heat up. Some of us, however, don’t have the patience for that. Luckily, the Stagg EKG is able to hold a set temperature for 60 minutes before shutting off for safety. We dig this feature because we have a tendency to walk off as the water heats up, only to come back to water that has come to room temperature again. You’ll never have to heat water twice with the Stagg EKG.

PID Controller

One of the more tech-savvy features of the Stagg EKG is its internal PID controller. PID is a fancy electrical engineering acronym for a type of system that reaches a desired setpoint in a very efficient way. The Stagg EKG’s PID controller lets it achieve and maintain the temperature setpoint so that you can spend less time waiting on your coffee and more time enjoying it.

High Power

Although the kettle base seems small, it is capable of using a shocking 1200 watts of power to heat up your water. This power rivals most espresso machines and will get you from fill-up to brewed cup in no time at all.

Style

The Stagg EKG is made by Fellow Products, a coffee company known for their attention to design without neglecting functionality. The Stagg EKG is not only a work of art, but comes in a variety of styles. With three unique styles to choose from, there is a Stagg EKG that will fit anyone’s kitchen.

Matte Black

Matte black is the original color for the Stagg kettle line. It’s what was offered during the original Kickstarter campaign and we’re glad to see it made a reappearance in the EKG line. The matte black look perfectly compliments many of the other great Fellow products, like the Atmos coffee canister. It’s a great fit if you have a modern kitchen.

Polished Copper

The polished copper look is definitely more classic. It is perfect for anyone who has copper pots and pans hanging on the wall. We love the copper look because it’s classy and sophisticated while keeping the minimalist nature of the Stagg EKG. As a heads up—the mirror finish can get dirty easily, so you’ll want to make sure you clean it regularly to keep it shining.

Polished Steel

A very safe option for the Stagg EKG Kettle is the stainless steel look. It’s guaranteed to fit in with any of your kitchen appliances or decor. We love the polished steel for its mirror finish but it can also get dirty pretty easily. Make sure you’re polishing it up every so often to make it look like new.

Sizes

The Stagg EKG Kettle comes in two different sizes: 0.9L and 0.6L. We prefer the 0.9L because it gives us the ability to brew larger volumes of coffee, especially if we’re having company. The 0.6L size ideal if you’re not making more than a few cups of coffee or tea at a time.

Stagg EKG vs. Stagg EKG+

The Stagg EKG Kettle comes in two different variants. There’s the original EKG and the EKG+. The biggest difference between the two options is the EKG+ is a smart device. You can remotely turn on/off your kettle, keep track of your recipes, and connect to the Acaia Brewbar Tablet App via Bluetooth. Most of these features are probably not needed for the average coffee drinker, but if you’re a super coffee nerd like us or run a cafe, it could be worth the extra cash to upgrade to the EKG+.
